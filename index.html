Living in France

Moving to France had been a dream for my parents for years, and finally in February of 2003, we made the move! We went from a suburb of a city Nottingham, England to a tiny hamlet in rural France called Chamberaud, in the Creuse region. Of the four of us, only Mum spoke French; my brother and I being young, became fluent in the space of about 6 months. It took a while longer for our Dad, and we took great pleasure in correcting his French, while he claimed he was just "checking you're paying attention" Our grandparents also moved to France in 2006. Everyone still lives there today; it has been nearly 18 years for my parents and brother!


Winters in La Creuse

Winter was always fun for us! We would often have snow, and made lots of snow angels, snowmen, but more importantly, sledding! Opposite our house was a short steep hill that we always sledded down. One year some family and close friends came for a surprise visit. A few hours after their arrival, it started snowing, and continued throughout the night. As kids, we were thrilled, the parents, not so much! We were constantly in and out, the floor was covered in newspaper to attempt to soak up the melting snow, there was gloves and clothes drying on radiators everywhere.


There were a few notable winters, but the worst was 2007. The snow came down so thick and heavy, and then it froze. The trees were so laden I don't know how they didn't break. Summer's fantastic too!

That's enough about winter, summers were always amazing. In fact the year we moved there was a heatwave in August, with temperatures reaching a whopping 41°C, or 104°F. These were the highest temperatures in 50 years!

Summers were usually filled with lots of exploring the local woods. I spent many days and hours biking and discovering new trails. All roads lead to Rome, so I never got lost. My brother and I occasionally helped the local farmers move cows from one field to another, which was always good fun, and something that we would never have had a chance to do in England! Some of our friends also owned a farm. We learned to milk the cows, pick chicken eggs and drive tractors. My friend Aurore could drive a Jeep by the time she was 11 (yep, even on the roads sometimes from one field to another, oops!)
